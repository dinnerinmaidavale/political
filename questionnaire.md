# Questionnaire

## Question Types

The questionnaire will use:

- Agree / disagree questions
- Multiple choice questions
- Ranking questions
- Scenario questions
- Trade-off questions

## Answer Scale

For agree/disagree questions:

1. Strongly disagree
2. Disagree
3. Slightly disagree
4. Neutral / unsure
5. Slightly agree
6. Agree
7. Strongly agree

## Question Design Rules

Questions should not mention parties.

Questions should avoid obvious political slogans.

Questions should reveal values indirectly.

Questions should be easy to understand.

## Example Questions

### Affordability

1. A good government should make everyday life cheaper before anything else.

2. It matters more that people can afford rent, bills and food than whether the economy looks strong on paper.

3. A country is failing if working people cannot afford a decent home.

### Safety

4. Feeling safe in your area is one of the most important parts of a good life.

5. The government should focus more on stopping harm before it happens than punishing people after.

6. Courts should deal with violent and sexual crimes much faster than they do now.

### Trust

7. Politicians should not be allowed to earn large amounts of money from second jobs.

8. Political parties should not take large donations from billionaires or private companies.

9. News organisations should clearly show who funds them.

10. If a politician knowingly lies, there should be serious consequences.

### Community

11. A strong community matters more than a fast-growing economy.

12. People should feel they belong where they live.

13. Public spaces should bring people together, not just serve businesses.

### Immigration and Identity

14. Immigration should be managed in a way that protects both public services and community stability.

15. People who move to Britain should be supported to learn the language and understand local life.

16. A country can be diverse and still have a strong shared identity.

### Economy

17. The richest people should pay more if public services are struggling.

18. Big companies should have less influence over government.

19. Small businesses should get more support than large corporations.

### Housing

20. Homes should be treated more like places to live than investments.

21. Councils should be allowed to build far more homes.

22. Local people should have more say over what gets built near them.

### Freedom

23. People should be free to say unpopular things, even if others find them offensive.

24. Government should not monitor people unless there is a clear reason.

25. Privacy matters even when surveillance might make life more convenient.

### Climate

26. The government should protect nature even when it slows down some business activity.

27. Clean energy should be treated as a national priority.

28. Future generations should matter as much as people alive today.

### Ranking Example

Rank these from most important to least important:

- lower bills
- safer streets
- better NHS
- cheaper housing
- lower immigration
- cleaner environment
- more freedom
- honest politicians
